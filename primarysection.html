Curriculum

Our teachers and administration reflect upon and refine our curriculum to ensure we are meeting the needs of students. Just as our world and our students are ever-evolving, our teaching practices evolve. Alongside the academic subjects the regular activity of the school includes offered as separate class-periods:

  • Life Skills-in-Education (LIE): Different methods that are used to enhance Life Skills in students are: Worksheets, Class discussions, Brainstorming, Role plays, Special Assembly based on Life Skills topics, Case studies, Storytelling, Debates etc.
  • Library Reading in Education (LRIE): Class Library and the exhaustive School library is a regular period for students and diligently carried out. A Library card is issued to ensure students are taking and reading books.
  • Theatre-in-Education (TIE): Dramatics and enactment integrated with languages, sciences, social sciences, mathematics and IT. It encourages children to ask questions, make decisions and explore sensitive issues creatively.
  • Art-in-Education (AIE): Integration of creative art and craft skills to understand, analyze and express concepts in languages, mathematics, science and social science.

Our aim is to educate young people to lead safe and healthy lives and achieve great success. Cultural education subjects like art and design, dance, drama and music spark creativity and encourage inquisitiveness, discipline and determination.

Methodology

Developing academic and social-emotional skills for success in future learning

In the Middle School, we strive to bring our mission to life for grade 5 through 8 students so that they have the academic and social-emotional skills needed for success in future learning and life.

Small classes and caring, experienced staff make all the difference

Teachers are subject specialists with extensive experience. Collaboration ensures students see connections between subjects and real-world applications.

Optimum student-teacher ratio enables more engaging hands-on activities, field trips and enriched learning.
